# Venture Taranaki Engagement

Operational skill for productive, high-signal engagement with Venture Taranaki (Te Puna Umanga) and its programmes. Optimised for a deep-tech AgriTech / sovereign edge founder based in or focused on Taranaki.

## Core Principles

- Treat Venture Taranaki as a strategic partner, not a grant ATM.
- Every interaction should leave Nick / the Enterprise Advisor with a clear next action and evidence of founder seriousness.
- Prefer discovery conversations over pitches in the first 30 days.
- Always respect the incorporation timing: company must be live (or clearly on a firm date) before formal ScaleUp or deeper support pathways.

## When to Load

- Preparing or following up emails / meetings with Nick or any Enterprise Advisor
- Mapping the 10 named Taranaki targets
- Booking discovery conversations
- Applying for ScaleUp grant, PowerUp Business Ready, PowerUp Accelerator, or RBP vouchers
- Requesting warm intros to TRC, iwi organisations, dairy co-ops, or civil construction firms
- PowerUp Investment Month participation

## Standard Playbooks

### 1. Pre-incorporation / Just-sent email follow-up
- Confirm incorporation date (8 August 2026) and that all arrangements are underway.
- List concrete interim progress (hardware procured, live farm streams, IP readiness, wedge chosen).
- Ask specifically for: ScaleUp grant path, Business Ready next intake, PowerUp Accelerator next cohort, warm intros to TRC and relevant iwi contacts.
- Offer a short call or one-pager once incorporated (or sooner if useful).

### 2. Target mapping (Days 1–30)
Work with Nick to produce a living list of ~10 named targets:
- Taranaki Regional Council + NPDC / STDC / SDC
- 2–3 dairy co-op / supplier groups
- 2 civil construction firms with multi-party compliance pain
- 1 iwi organisation with land/water interests
- 1 existing rural SaaS / service provider as white-label candidate

Goal of discovery conversations: hear the compliance / data-sovereignty pain in their words; identify 3 who would take a paid pilot.

### 3. Discovery conversation structure (not a pitch)
1. Open with context (sovereign offline AI for regulated rural compliance).
2. Ask about their current evidence / monitoring / consent process and where it breaks.
3. Listen for budgeted pain (especially council catchment programmes and Freshwater Farm Plan evidence).
4. Only then lightly surface the working demo unit and pilot offer.
5. Close with clear next step (on-site demo or LOI discussion).

### 4. Funding & programme asks
- ScaleUp grant ($5k) — use for demo hardware + enclosure certification. Prepare simple application materials.
- PowerUp Business Ready — ask for next intake date.
- PowerUp Accelerator — apply for next cohort (March showcase timing is ideal for a later raise).
- RBP Capability Development Fund vouchers — confirm GST + trading status eligibility after incorporation.
- Investment Month sessions — attend all available (Aug, Oct, Feb, May).

## HITL & Guardrails

- Never send external emails or meeting requests without founder approval of the exact text.
- Never claim formal “support” or “endorsement” from Venture Taranaki unless explicitly given.
- Keep all grant and programme information current; flag when information may be outdated post-Callaghan changes.
- All grant applications are drafted only; founder submits.

## Output Standards

Every engagement artefact (email draft, target list, conversation notes, grant outline) must include:
- Date
- Intended recipient / purpose
- Explicit “Ready for founder review / send” status
- Link back to the current 30/60/90 priorities

## Success Criteria

- Founder has a clear, prioritised list of next conversations and asks.
- Every interaction with Venture Taranaki advances either legitimacy, pipeline, or non-dilutive funding.
- No over-promising of product capability or timelines.
