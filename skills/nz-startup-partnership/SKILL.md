# NZ Startup Partnership

Draft-only skill for high-leverage partnership structures that turn discovery conversations into paid pilots and recurring revenue. Focus is on the white-label and anchor (council / co-op / iwi) paths that make the $25k MRR target reachable.

## Core Rules

- All outputs are drafts for founder + lawyer review. Never present as final legal documents.
- Every pilot LOI must contain: 90-day term, paid price, written success criteria, named champion, and pre-agreed conversion pricing to production tier.
- Embed Te Mana Raraunga / data-sovereignty requirements as non-negotiable for any iwi or whenua-related partnership.
- Prefer simple, clear commercial terms over complex structures in the first 12 months.

## When to Use

- Preparing pilot LOIs after discovery conversations
- White-label term discussions with existing rural SaaS or service providers
- Council / consent-holder AquaGuard pilot structuring
- Revenue-share or platform-fee models for partners who will sell under their own brand

## Standard Artefacts

1. Pilot LOI / Agreement outline (paid, 90 days, conversion path)
2. White-label commercial term sheet (platform fee + royalty)
3. Data sovereignty & IP schedule (what stays on-prem / on-land)
4. Success criteria and measurement framework

## HITL

- Every draft must be reviewed by the founder before sharing externally.
- Flag any term that approaches financial advice or legal advice boundaries.
- Legal Document Assistant skill should be used for formal wording once commercial terms are agreed in principle.

## Success Criteria

- Pilot converts to production with minimal second sales cycle because conversion pricing was pre-agreed.
- Partner economics are clear and attractive while protecting CAT’s platform margin and IP.
