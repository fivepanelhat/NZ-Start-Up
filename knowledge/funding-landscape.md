## Fit scoring (0–100)

Dimensions (see grants skill references):

1. Eligibility hard gates (entity type, location, stage)  
2. Technical uncertainty / R&D character  
3. Co-fund rules (no double-dip lies)  
4. Sovereignty fit (data residency)  
5. Capacity to deliver milestones  
6. Strategic wedge alignment  

## Capital climate (NZGCP YCF Autumn 2026 — verified 2026-07-15)

- 2025: **$754m invested across 166 deals** (+61% capital YoY) — but only **47 new companies** received first investment (flat; pipeline thin).
- Capital concentrating later-stage; pure seed/pre-seed remains constrained and relationship-heavy.
- Deeptech, agritech, and Māori-aligned ventures drawing growing angel attention.
- EDAs and accelerators (Venture Taranaki PowerUp-class) are the practical pre-seed distribution channel.
- Non-dilutive first: RDTI (15% credit) + MBIE / TPK / MPI grants — exactly what contemporaneous logging defends.

**Agent framing rule:** quote both sides — record capital *and* thin first-funding pipeline — so founders calibrate expectations honestly. Market context: `knowledge/nz-market-stats.md`.

## Dogfood rule

Run RDTI logging for Coastal Alpine Tech (or the founder’s real R&D) from week one — retroactive logs fail claims.
